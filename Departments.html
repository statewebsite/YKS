Agricultural Science Department

Agricultural Science as a full elective program was culled from the Science Department in the 2012/2013 academic year with thirty-five (35) students. Currently, with a seven-member staff strength, the Elective subjects being offered by the Agric Science students are:

·         General Agriculture (as the main subject)

·         Crop Husbandry/Horticulture (as an option)

·         Animal Husbandry (as an option)

·         Physics OR Elective Mathematics (as a science subject)

·         Chemistry (as a science subject).    

HEADS OF DEPARTMENT

·         Mr. Joseph Siaw (2012 – 2018) 

·         Mr. Abraham Kpabitey (2018 – date.)    

SCHOOL FARM/PROJECTS 
·         The department manages a school farm for demonstration purposes. The crops under cultivation currently are carrots, pepper, groundnuts, cowpea and maize. 

·         In close collaboration with the Mango Growers Association, Somanya (who have their offices on the school land), the school can boast of over seventy (70) mango trees on the school farm. 

·         The school also has an Oil Palm Plantation which started in 2017, and currently has over 60 trees.

·         Another project the department has embarked upon is to develop woodlots around the school field and other key installations on campus. This is for several purposes, including aesthetic, wind breaks, among others.    

ACHIEVEMENTS 
·         Blue Skies School Farm of the Year Competition:

−        2015 – 2nd out of 30 schools nationwide 

−        2016 – 3rd out of 30 schools nationwide 

−        2017 – 2nd out of 60 schools nationwide 

−        2019 – 4th out of 80 schools nationwide 

·         For these lofty performances, the organizers, Blue Skies Gh. Ltd., presented a total of nine (9) desktop computers to the department. 

·         In 2016, the first HOD, Mr. Joseph Siaw was awarded the Best Agric Science teacher of the year. He received a laptop computer.

·         The school also won the first prize (crop division) at the SHS level during the 2015 edition of Farmers’ Day Celebration held at Nkurakan. 

Business Department

Being a business biased school, the Business Department was the first to be established with the inception of the school. It is with this department that the school was crowned with all the glory throughout the O’Level days, through SSSCE and now WASSCE. Some of the courses studied at the then O’Level were:
·         Commerce 

·         Business Methods and Office Practice 

·         Typewriting 

·         Financial Accounting 

·         Economics, among others. 

With the introduction of the SSSCE however, some of the courses were realigned. Today, the following courses are studied in the department:
·         Business Management 

·         Typewriting 

·         Clerical Office Duties 

·         Cost Accounting 

·         Financial Accounting 

·         Economics 

·         Mathematics (Elective)    

HEADS OF DEPARTTMENT 
Mr. James Owiredu Yeboah, Mr. Atiadeve Ernest were part of the long list of prominent names to have headed the prestigious business department. More recently, the following were the heads of the department: 
·         Mr. Evans Teye (2003 – 2007) 

·         Mr. Samuel Yao Okuada (2007 - 2018) 

·         Ms. Priscilla Kadiga Aopare (2018 – date.)   

The department has a staff strength of eleven (11) teachers as of July 2021.   

 TROPHIES 
·         Winner (2008 and 2010), Inter School Business Quiz       Competition

·         Winner, (2010,2012,2016) Inter Department Quiz Competitions

General Arts Department

Appropriately nicknamed the Social Science Department, the department is largely responsible for shaping the ‘critical thinking’ abilities of students under its umbrella. The department was established in the late 1990s with the following subjects, a few of which were added on later as the numbers grew and the options began to vary… 
·         Economics 

·         Geography 

·         Government 

·         History 

·         Social Studies 

·         Music 

·         Christian Religious Studies (CRS)   
Currently the largest traditional department in the school, the department has thirty one (31) staff members, five different General Art Classes with varied combinations to meet the ever-changing needs of students who pass through the department.

·         Mr. Kofi Amai (2013 - 2019) 

·         Mr. Felix Kwesi Tetteh (2019 – date.)   

PROGRAMS 

·         The department represents the school at the annual Independence Day Quiz competitions and has performed creditably at all such competitions. 

·         Periodically, field trips and excursions and local studies are held to open the horizon of students and give them a more practical view of the theories they learn in class.  

Home Economics Department

The Home Economics program was introduced in the school in 1990 with the inception of the SSS system. There are three main areas of study under the program, viz; 
·         Food and Nutrition 

·         Clothing and Textiles 

·         Management in Living 

Following initial hitches of not having a well-equipped department for a course with heavy practical works, the then Board Chairperson, Mrs. Batsa, and the then headmistress, Mrs. D. L. Ametefe appealed to several entities for assistance. In July 1998, the Danish Development Agency (DANIDA) handed over a modern, well equipped department that stands in the heart of the school.   Currently, there are five (5) permanent staff members taking charge of the close to four hundred (400) entrepreneurs in the making.  

HEADS OF DEPARTMENT

·         Madam Adelaide Debrah (2000 - 2008) 

·         Madam Mabel Lomotey (2008 - 2015) 

·         Madam Magdalene Fudoglo (2017 - date)   

ACHIEVEMENTS AND PROGRAMS 
·         The department, on several occasions has either won, or performed creditably at the Cook Art and Sewing Competitions, including winning the very recent one organized in 2018 during the Municipal Cultural Festival. 

·         The 2019 batch of Food and Nutrition students scored an impressive 100% in the WASSCE, after a few years of failing to make that enviable mark. 

·         The current head of department, in collaboration with the teachers have established the Home Economics Students Association (HESA) in the school, to better organize the students from within the department.

Science Department

With the introduction of the Senior Secondary School System in 1990 came the Science Department. Initially, it was treated as a minor course in a school that was openly Business biased. Those times, only a handful of students, largely no more than ten, enrolled into the then “science department”.   
As of July 2021, the YIKROSEC Science Department proudly boasts of over One Hundred and Sixty (160) General Science students with a staff strength of twenty-two (22), made up of thirteen (13) teachers of the General Science discipline; five (5) of the Information Communication Technology (ICT) sub-department; and four (4) from the Health, Physical Education Recreation & Sports (HPERS) sub-department, after a 2019 realignment policy from the Ghana Education Service (GES) brought ICT and PE under the umbrella of the Science Department.   
The elective subjects available for the science students are as follows: 

·         Biology 

·         Chemistry 

·         Physics 

·         Elective ICT 

·         Physical Education 

·         Elective Mathematics  

HEADS OF DEPARTMENT 
As a department that largely struggled to get established, and which had its very few students pairing with other courses to get some space, several committed teachers headed this “department-less” department including; 
·         Mr. Agbley (1990 – 1995) 

·         Mr. Kom-Teye (1995 – 1998) 

·         Mr. Paul Teye O. Tsawe (1998 – 2019) 

·         Mr. Joseph Matey Terkper (2019 – date.) 

In 2004, the department took its first set of Science Students in Year One, who would go on to complete in 2007 as the first batch of a properly constituted YIKROSEC Science Department. This new department had Mr. P.T.O Tsawe still heading it till 2019 when he handed over to Mr. Joseph Matey Terpker.   

THE YIKROSEC SCIENCE STUDENTS ASSOCIATION (YIKSSA) 
This is an association of students belonging to the YIKROSEC Science department under the tutelage and direction of the department’s teachers. It was established in 2007 through the leadership of Mr. Joseph Terpker, the current HOD. The motivation for the establishment is to arouse the interest of students (especially the girl child) in the study of science as a discipline through programs like the YIKSSA Science Week Celebrations at which exhibitions are made, seminars, symposia, among many other such programs. This association, YIKSSA, is also responsible for preparing students for the annual National Science and Maths Quiz, the Energy Quiz, and lately, the Renewable Energy Competitions – competitions at which the school has performed steadily well.  

ACHIEVEMENTS/ACCOLADES/PROJECTS 
·         NATIONAL SCIENCE & MATHS QUIZ 

−        Between 2013, when the school took its first shot at the prestigious competition till 2015, the department merely represented without any significant glories. 

−        By 2016, a team of teachers from both the Maths and Science departments with enough experience from the previous years was put together. In both 2016 and 2017, the school placed third at the Regional Competition. 

−        In 2018, 2019 and 2021, the school inched closer by placing 2nd at all those competitions at the regional level. 


·         ENERGY QUIZ 

−        In 2019, the school participated in the Energy Quiz, went through the ranks placing 1st throughout the preliminary stage, group stage, and the quarter finals, but fell off at the Semis. ·         RENEWABLE ENERGY COMPETITION 

−        In 2021, the school participated in the Renewable Energy Competition which was open to all schools in the country to present a Project on the usage of renewable energy. Ten (10) of the best schools selected in the Eastern Region for Project Presentation included YIKROSEC, which did a presentation on Wind Powered Water Pump to store water for use within the school community.

PROJECTS 
·         The department looks forward to getting enough resources to assist it build this novel project of a Wind Powered Water Pump off the heels of the Renewable Energy Project; 
·         Also, the department looks to partner with relevant organizations to establish a mini Weather Station on Campus.

Visual Arts Department

The Visual Arts department in YIKROSEC pre-dates the current Senior Secondary/Senior High School system. It became a full-fledged department in the late 1990s. The department has nine staff members – a female and eight males. Presently, students in the department have the opportunity to study the following Elective subjects: 
·         General Knowledge in Arts 

·         Graphic Design 

·         Textiles 

·         Leatherworks    

HEADS OF DEPARTMENT 
Since the department was established, a few persons have served in acting and caretaker capacities, notable among them is Mr. Benedict Crabbe. The substantive HODs are: 

·         Mr. Godwin Senyo Fudzi (2005 - 2017) 

·         Mr. Dwommoh Frimpong Franklin (2017 – date.)   

ACHIEVEMENTS AND PROGRAMS 

·         The department has been consistent in its top-class performances at the annual West African Senior School Certificate Examinations (WASSCE) over the years with series of 100% records! This culminated in the department being awarded with a laptop computer at the 2019 Independence Day Celebration for this consistency. 

·         The department also participates in several Arts and Cultural Festivals including the 2006 edition held in Donkorkrom, and Aburi (2019). ARTSOC, YIKROSEC’s chapter of the Arts Society has been in charge of fine artist’s impressions and other mural works of art that help beautify the walls of buildings on campus. Others like periodic painting works on campus are proudly undertaken by the students of the Society, in a bid to sharpen their skills.
